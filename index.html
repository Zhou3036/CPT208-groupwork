1. Motivation & Research

Project Motivation

Our group chose the Digital Heritage track – specifically Suzhou's Maple Bridge – because we identified a clear mismatch between the site's cultural richness and how visitors experience it today. Professor Chen Wei, a historian, needs accurate, citation‑grounded reconstructions for his teaching, yet existing resources are either too simplistic or visually unreliable. Li Wei, a tech‑savvy father, wants his daughter to enjoy history through play, but current offerings rely on dense text and static displays that neither engage a child nor fit a busy weekend schedule. Reviewing four academic papers and four commercial products (e.g., museum audio guides, heritage apps), we found three common strengths: visual appeal, factual coverage, and multi‑language support. However, three critical weaknesses remained: (1) lack of verifiable historical references for experts, (2) no gamified elements to retain younger users, and (3) poor responsiveness on mobile devices. To address these gaps, we designed “Echoes of Maple Bridge” – a playful, XR‑ready web app that serves both personas: an interactive timeline with cited sources for Professor Chen, and a stamp‑collection quiz with instant feedback for Li Wei and his daughter. By balancing scholarly depth with family‑friendly interaction, our project demonstrates that human‑centered heritage tools can be both rigorous and delightful.

5. Evaluation & Reflection

Usability Testing Results

We conducted tests with 3 users (1 Professor, 2 Students).

  • User 1: Found the AR feature intuitive but complained about lighting conditions.
  • User 2: Loved the poetry puzzle but got stuck on the second level.
  • User 3: Suggested adding a "Skip Tutorial" button for returning users.

Social, Ethical & AI Reflection

Social Impact: Our project promotes cultural heritage preservation. However, we must ensure that digital representations do not distort historical facts.

Ethical Consideration: We respected user privacy by not collecting location data permanently. All data is processed locally where possible.

AI Reflection: While AI accelerated our coding process, we manually verified all historical dates and CSS logic to ensure accuracy and brand consistency. We acknowledge that AI lacks cultural nuance, so human review was critical.
