ECHOES OF MAPLE BRIDGE

Group A2-6

About The Project

Echoes of Maple Bridge is a digital heritage project that recreates the historical landscape of Suzhou's iconic Maple Bridge. Using XR (Extended Reality) and location-based technologies, users can "touch" the bridge across different time periods — from Zhang Ji's poetic "Mooring by Maple Bridge" in the Tang Dynasty, through its prosperity as a commercial hub in the Ming and Qing Dynasties, to its present-day status as a landmark of Suzhou.

Our mission is to transform Maple Bridge from a static monument into a living history that users can walk through and experience.

User Personas

[Prof. Chen]

Professor Chen Wei

Age: 58 | Occupation: History Professor, Soochow University

Background

Lived in Suzhou for 30+ years, published 4 books on Grand Canal history. Brings students to Maple Bridge for fieldwork every semester. Owns collection of historical maps and poems dating back to Tang Dynasty.

Goals

Access historically accurate 3D reconstructions across dynasties. Use system as teaching tool during on-site classes. Contribute historical insights to improve accuracy.

Pain Points

Existing resources limited to 2D images - can't convey spatial relationships. Many reconstructions are "artistically pleasing" but historically inaccurate. Needs precise citations for each historical layer.

"A beautiful visualization means nothing if the history is wrong. I need to trust what I'm seeing."
