About The Project

Echoes of Maple Bridge is a digital heritage project that recreates the historical landscape of Suzhou's iconic Maple Bridge. Using XR (Extended Reality) and location-based technologies, users can "touch" the bridge across different time periods — from Zhang Ji's poetic "Mooring by Maple Bridge" in the Tang Dynasty, through its prosperity as a commercial hub in the Ming and Qing Dynasties, to its present-day status as a landmark of Suzhou.

Our mission is to transform Maple Bridge from a static monument into a living history that users can walk through and experience.

Historical Timeline

Tang Dynasty - Origin & Poetry

Approx. 8th Century

Zhang Ji's poem "Mooring by Maple Bridge at Night" transforms "Feng Bridge" into a cultural icon.

Ming & Qing - Commercial Peak

1368 - 1912

Becomes the national center for rice and bean distribution. The "Feng Hu" becomes the standard measure.

Qing Dynasty - Rebuilding & Resilience

1770 / 1860 / 1867

Rebuilt in 1770, destroyed in 1860, and reconstructed as the current granite arch bridge in 1867.

War Period - Heritage Protection

1937 - 1945

Patriots protect the famous stone stele from looting by creating replicas during the war.

Modern Restoration

Year 1984

Major restoration of the granite structure to preserve its historical appearance for future generations.

Digital Preservation

Year 2020

"Echoes of Maple Bridge" project launched using XR technology to digitally preserve and showcase the bridge's history.
